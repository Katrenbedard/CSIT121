Sesame Crusted Ahi-Tuna Steak

Recipe by Shinee Sesame Crusted Ahi Tuna

Tuna Steak Cooked Simple and Savory

Why I love this Recipe!

As a pescaterian, I'm limited in what I can eat. Coupled with the fact that I'm not a good chef, my usual go to food has something to do with eggs The main reason I haven't gone full on vegetarian is my love of seafood. Especially that of Tuna steak. When not eating it sashmi style, I prefer my steak to be coated with seasame seeds and cooked as rare as possible. Like the fish should still be flopping around a bit. The reciple below not only shows the best way to cook such a fish but also a delicious sauce that can be used to exemplify the Tuna's already fresh taste!

Sauce Ingredients

  • 1/2 Cup Fresh Cilantro Leaves
  • 1-inch Fresh Ginger
  • 1-2 Garlic Cloves
  • 2 Tablespoons Soy Sauce
  • 2 Tablespoons Water
  • 2 Teaspoons Toasted Sesame Oil
  • 1 Teaspoon Brown Sugar or Honey

Tuna Steak Ingredients

  • 4(4-6oz) Ahi Tuna Steaks
  • 1 Teaspoon Coarse Kosher Salt
  • Freshly Ground Black Pepper
  • 1/4 Cup Toasted Sesame Seeds
  • 1-2 Tablespoons Avocado Oil

Preperation

  1. Season Tuna Steaks with Salt and Pepper, and coat the steaks with sesame seeds on both sides, pressing them down to stick to the fish
  2. Heat a medium stainless steel, or cast iron skillet, over medium high heat. Add oil to the heated skillet
  3. When the oil is shummering hot, add tuna steaks and sear for 1-1.5 minutes. Flip and sear for another 1-1.5 minutes. (Feel free to cook a little longer to your desired doneness)
  4. At the last 30-seconds of cooking, add about half of the dressing into the skillet with tuna steaks. Cook for a few seconds, flip and sear for another couple seconds.
  5. Remove the steaks onto a clean plate, slice and serve!
