Notable Films

Roger Corman and Edgar Allen Poe

Between the years 1960-1964, Director Roger Corman made eight different adaptations of Edgar Allen Poe poems and short stories. Of the eight, Vincent Price starred in seven of them (the exception being The Premature Burial (1962) starring Ray Milland). The first of the films was House of Usher (1960), based on the short story of the same name. The eight films ranged in tone as The Raven’s (1963) comedic style was a far cry from the seriousness of House of Usher and The Masque of the Red Death (1964). Tales of Terror (1962) was different than the other seven as it was an anthology of three sequences that fit four different short stories into it. The first being Morella, the second is a combination of The Black Cat and The Cask of Amontillado, and The Facts in the Case of M. Valdemar. Though different stories, each of the seven films had one two things in common: Vincent Price and Low-Budget Effects.

The Comedy of Terrors(1964)

Directed by Jacques Tourneur, The Comedy of Terrors had a cast of prominent horror actors. Alongside Price was Boris Karloff, Peter Lorre, and Basil Rathbone. Taking place in the New England town of Gilead during the 19th century, Price plays the part of a corrupt funeral owner who isn’t afraid of making a few sacrifices to keep his business profitable.

Last Man on Earth(1964)

Based on the 1954 novel ‘I am Legend’ by Richard Matheson, Directors Sidney Salkow and Ubaldo Ragona The Last Man on Earth as a science fiction horror film. Price, the star, plays the part of a man immune to a vampiric plague that has riddled the rest of the world.
