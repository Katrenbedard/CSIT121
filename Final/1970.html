Notable Films

The Abominable Dr.Phibes and Dr. Phibes Rises Again(1971,1972)

Directed by Robert Fuest, The Abominable Dr. Phibes(1971) tells a story of a grieving, mutilated man and his quest for revenge. Taking inspiration from the Old Testament, Price’s character slashes through a list of victims who he believes is responsible for the death of his wife. The sequel a year later, while no longer a tale of revenge, doesn’t hold back when it comes to Phibes ‘abominable’ nature.

Theater of Blood(1973)

Dr. Phibes wasn’t the only Price character obessed with revenge. In Theater of Blood(1973), directed by Douglas Hickox, Price plays as the a snubbed Shakesparian actor Edward Lionheart. With a flare for the dramatics, it’s easy to see how comedy mingles with the bloody when Price is behind the knife.
